# Phase 12.6: Existing AI Autonomy Distillation - Research

**Researched:** 2026-04-25
**Domain:** Decision-time teacher distillation, calibrated decision-head learning, shadow-mode runtime rollout
**Confidence:** HIGH on repo fit, MEDIUM-HIGH on rollout thresholds

## Summary

Phase 12.6 should not introduce a new AI service or a second trading brain. The best-fit architecture for GoldBot is to distill the current teacher chain into a learned decision head inside the existing ensemble path, while keeping `DecisionGovernor`, `StrategyManager`, `EventService`, `RiskManager`, and broker-side execution as externally authoritative runtime guards.

The correct sequence is:

1. capture lossless decision-time teacher snapshots from the current stack,
2. materialize an offline distillation dataset with hierarchical labels,
3. train a calibrated `BUY/HOLD/SELL` decision head on decision-state features,
4. compare it walk-forward against the current champion,
5. roll it out in `shadow`, then `agreement_guarded`, then `primary_with_challenger`.

## Architecture Patterns

### Pattern 1: Teacher snapshots before broker side effects

- Capture one append-only snapshot per decision tick.
- Snapshot boundary should sit after raw AI signal generation and before broker execution.
- Persist teacher outputs, downstream guard outcomes, and later realized execution outcomes as separate blocks.

### Pattern 2: Hierarchical labels, not flat HOLD collapse

- Keep `preliminary_action`, `policy_action`, `final_action`, `block_stage`, and `block_codes` separate.
- This lets the student distinguish genuine abstain from governance/calendar/strategy/risk/broker blocks.

### Pattern 3: In-process decision head

- The learned head belongs inside `EnsemblePredictor`.
- It should consume decision-state features, output calibrated `SELL/HOLD/BUY` probabilities, and feed the existing governor instead of replacing it.

### Pattern 4: Shadow-first rollout

- Champion and candidate should evaluate the same tick with the same `df`, `mtf_data`, spread, and account snapshot.
- Only one selected signal may continue into the existing `StrategyManager -> RiskManager -> OrderManager` chain.
- Promotion should be staged by trade/sample quality, not random traffic percentage.

## Don't Hand-Roll

- Do not build a separate microservice or sidecar for the autonomy head.
- Do not create a second calibration stack or threshold format.
- Do not reconstruct the training dataset only from flattened `signals` or human-readable reason strings.
- Do not invent a new audit store when `GovernanceDecision.evaluation_summary` can carry rollout details.
- Do not expand the action space beyond `SELL/HOLD/BUY` in this phase.

## Common Pitfalls

- Flattening every blocked trade into `HOLD`.
- Capturing post-fill fields as model inputs instead of realized outcomes.
- Training on in-sample teacher outputs instead of walk-forward / out-of-fold decision states.
- Letting the head relearn raw market features instead of decision-state features.
- Promoting on accuracy while HOLD dominates.
- Allowing runtime bypass of `DecisionGovernor`, `StrategyManager`, calendar blocks, or `RiskManager`.

## Recommended Acceptance Metrics

- `policy_action` macro-F1 on `BUY/HOLD/SELL`
- directional precision/recall on non-HOLD samples
- Brier score, log loss, ECE, reliability bins
- same-window profit factor, expectancy, drawdown, trade count
- disagreement-bucket quality
- `guard_bypass_count = 0`
- shadow sample/trade count sufficient for promotion review
